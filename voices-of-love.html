Your song. Your voice. Your story.

A participatory music experience where individuals request meaningful songs, join the performance, and share the memories and experiences connected to the music.

♪
The Program

What Is Voices of Love?

Voices of Love begins with a simple invitation: What song would you like to hear?

Participants may request a favorite song, a song connected to a meaningful memory, a song that brings comfort, or music they would like to dedicate to someone else.

GYCO musicians then prepare and perform the selected music whenever possible.

Participants are encouraged to do more than listen. They may sing with the musicians, clap, move with the rhythm, play a simple instrument, dedicate the song, or share why the music matters to them.

Why It Matters

A Song Is Often Connected to More Than Music

A familiar song may remind someone of home.

It may bring back a family memory.

It may honor a loved one.

It may represent faith, hope, strength, joy, or comfort.

It may help someone express something that is difficult to explain with ordinary words.

By allowing participants to choose the music, Voices of Love places their experiences at the center of the performance.

The Process

How It Works

Step 1

Request a Song

Participants submit the name of a song they would like GYCO to perform. They may also explain why the song is meaningful, although sharing a personal story is always optional.

Step 2

GYCO Prepares the Music

GYCO students and musicians review the request and determine whether the song can be arranged or performed.

Step 3

Experience the Performance

The requested song may be performed live or recorded, depending on the location, program, and available musicians.

Step 4

Join the Music

Participants may sing, clap, play simple instruments, follow lyrics, or enjoy the performance in their preferred way.

Step 5

Share the Experience

Participants may share a reflection, memory, written message, photograph, or short response about what the experience meant to them.

The Circle

One Song Can Continue the Circle

A participant requests a song.

GYCO students learn and prepare it.

The participant joins the performance.

The music creates a shared memory.

The participant may share that experience with someone else.

That story may inspire another song request, performance, or message.

This is the Circle of Love in action.

After Your Experience

Your Experience Matters

After participating, individuals and families may choose to share:

  • What song they requested and why it was meaningful
  • How they participated and how the experience made them feel
  • A memory connected to the music
  • A message for the musicians
  • A message for another patient or family

Sharing is always optional. Participants do not need to provide medical information or personal details to take part.
