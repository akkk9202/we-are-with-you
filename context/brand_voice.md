# Brand voice

## The core

Name: **WE ARE WITH YOU** (all caps when used as the brand mark).
Organization: **Greater Youth Collaborative Opus (GYCO)**.
Tagline: **"Even Here. Even Now. We Are With You."**

The voice is **warm, calm, human, and present**. We are a companion, not a service provider. Every
line should feel like it was written by a caring person to another person in a hard moment.

## Principles

1. **Presence over promotion.** We say "we are with you," not "we offer solutions." No hype, no
   marketing superlatives, no urgency tactics.
2. **Dignity first.** The audience includes patients, grieving families, and people with
   disabilities. Never pity, never "inspiration," never clinical distance. People are participants,
   not beneficiaries.
3. **Plain and gentle.** Short sentences. Concrete nouns. Everyday words. Read it aloud — if it
   sounds like a brochure or a pitch deck, rewrite it.
4. **Invitational.** Prefer "you are invited," "when you're ready," "you may" over commands.
5. **Honest.** Don't promise what isn't live yet. If a form isn't ready, say "email us and we'll take
   it from there" (this pattern already exists on the contact page).
6. **Quiet where it matters.** NICU and end-of-life-adjacent copy is intentionally softer and
   stiller than the rest of the site. Match the room.

## Do / Don't

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "Even here, even now, we are with you." | "We're here to help you succeed!" |
| "You are not walking alone." | "Join thousands who…" |
| "Request a meaningful song for a loved one." | "Sign up now — limited spots!" |
| "When possible, students may prepare the song." | Over-promising guaranteed outcomes |
| "You are seen. You are remembered." | Pity framing ("these poor families") |

## Mechanics

- **Casing:** "WE ARE WITH YOU" as the mark; sentence case for body copy and most headings.
- **Tagline punctuation:** "Even Here. Even Now. We Are With You." (three beats, title-style) is the
  canonical form for the mark; lowercase "even here, even now, we are with you." is used inside warm
  closing paragraphs. Keep whichever form a given page already uses.
- **Bilingual:** English + Korean coverage is a feature (see Media). Preserve Korean text exactly
  (e.g., 나도) and its encoded URLs.
- **Names are load-bearing:** partner display names (e.g., "City of Hope Atlanta (CTCA)", "The
  America Wheat Mission (Milal)") are the agreed public names — don't shorten or "clean them up."

## When rewriting copy

Use `skills/content_rewrite_for_website.md`. Preserve meaning, keep it in `js/partners.js` /
`js/config.js` where the content lives, and never change a partner slug or a public partner name as a
side effect of editing prose.
