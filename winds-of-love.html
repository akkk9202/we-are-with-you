of Love

The music, people, projects, and stories of GYCO.

Winds of Love brings together the full journey of GYCO, from rehearsals and performances to service, teaching, research, leadership, and community partnerships.

The Program

What Is Winds of Love?

Winds of Love represents everything GYCO carries into the community. It includes the orchestra's music, but it also includes the learning, leadership, service, relationships, and projects that grow around the music.

Like wind, the impact of one experience can travel beyond the place where it began.

A rehearsal can prepare students for a senior community performance.

A performance can lead to a conversation.

A conversation can inspire a research question.

Research can improve the next program.

A student can turn that experience into a lesson for someone else.

Winds of Love tells that complete story.

♬
The Foundation

The Music of GYCO

Music remains the foundation of GYCO. Through music, students learn discipline, preparation, listening, communication, and teamwork.

Students participate in:

  • Orchestra rehearsals and chamber music
  • Community performances and seasonal concerts
  • Senior community visits
  • Collaborative and special outreach performances
  • Solo and small-ensemble opportunities
♥
Service

Music Shared With Purpose

GYCO students bring music into spaces where connection matters. Students are encouraged to spend time speaking with participants and learning from their experiences.

Community service may include:

  • Senior living communities
  • Hospitals and healthcare organizations
  • Ronald McDonald House
  • Family support organizations
  • Schools, faith communities, and community events
  • Seasonal outreach programs
Leadership

Students Help Build the Work

GYCO students are not limited to performing assigned music. Leadership is demonstrated through responsibility and service, not only through titles.

Plan & Lead

Students plan programs, lead rehearsals, teach participants, and mentor younger students.

Create & Design

Students create teaching videos, design websites, develop surveys, and prepare educational resources.

Connect & Represent

Students communicate with community partners, organize media, and represent GYCO at meetings and events.

Reflection

Learning From What We Do

GYCO students may study the experiences created through music and outreach. Research allows students to move beyond assuming a program was helpful — it teaches them to listen, collect feedback, analyze experiences, and improve.

They may explore questions such as:

  • How does familiar music affect mood?
  • What helps families feel comfortable participating?
  • Which music activities are most accessible?
  • How can student teaching improve?
  • What do seniors, patients, families, and staff value most?
