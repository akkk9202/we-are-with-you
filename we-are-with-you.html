Even Here. Even Now.

A student-led educational and community outreach movement built around music, encouragement, research, service, and human connection.

Why We Exist

No One Should Walk Through a Difficult Journey Alone

WE ARE WITH YOU began with a simple observation: people facing illness, isolation, uncertainty, or major life transitions often need more than a performance or a one-time act of service.

They need to feel seen.

They need opportunities to participate. They need messages that remind them that their experiences matter. They also need opportunities to become sources of hope for someone else.

WE ARE WITH YOU brings students, families, patients, caregivers, healthcare professionals, seniors, educators, and community members into one growing cycle of learning and encouragement.

Our Identity

More Than a Music Program

What It Is Not

WE ARE WITH YOU is not simply a concert series, volunteer club, or collection of unrelated projects.

What It Is

It is an educational philosophy that turns real experiences into opportunities to learn, create, connect, and give back.

Music was the first language through which we expressed that philosophy. The same approach now extends into research, leadership, educational resources, healthcare outreach, storytelling, and global collaboration.

Our Process

The Journey

Step One

Listen

Students begin by learning from a community rather than assuming what it needs.

Step Two

Connect

Students build relationships through conversation, music, creativity, and shared experiences.

Step Three

Create

Students develop performances, videos, workshops, surveys, resources, or educational projects.

Step Four

Reflect

Students examine what worked, what participants experienced, and what should improve.

Step Five

Pass It Forward

Participants are invited to share encouragement, knowledge, or opportunity with someone else.

Inclusion

Who Can Participate?

WE ARE WITH YOU is designed to include people of different ages, experiences, and abilities. No previous musical experience is required for many of our programs. Participation begins with a willingness to learn, connect, and share.

Students and Families Patients and Caregivers Healthcare Professionals Hospital Staff Senior Community Residents Educators and Mentors Volunteers Community Members Students in Underserved Schools Global Educational Partners Multicultural Families Special Education Settings
Our Goal

Hope Should Never End
With the Person Who Receives It

Our goal is not only to comfort people. It is to create opportunities for every participant to recognize that they also have something meaningful to offer.

A patient can become an encourager.

A beginner can become a mentor.

A student can become a researcher.

A family can become a community.
