The Program

What Is Beat & Breeze?

Beat & Breeze transforms music from something people watch into something they experience together.

Participants explore rhythm, breathing, movement, percussion, listening, and creative expression through accessible group activities. Sessions are designed so that people of different ages and musical abilities can participate comfortably.

The goal is not technical perfection. The goal is connection.

A simple rhythm can begin a conversation. A shared beat can help a family laugh together. A breathing activity can create a moment of calm. A small instrument can give someone the confidence to participate.

The Name

The Meaning Behind the Name

Beat

Rhythm, energy, participation, and the courage to make the first sound.

Breeze

Breath, movement, calmness, flexibility, and the way encouragement can travel from one person to another.

Together, Beat & Breeze creates a space where rhythm and breath become tools for connection.

Session Structure

What Happens During a Session?

Welcome

Welcome

Participants are introduced to the instruments and invited to join at their own comfort level.

Activity One

Rhythm Exploration

The group experiments with clapping, tapping, percussion instruments, and call-and-response patterns.

Activity Two

Breathing and Movement

Simple guided breathing and movement activities help participants slow down, focus, and connect with the group.

Activity Three

Family Music-Making

Participants create rhythms and musical patterns together rather than only following an instructor.

Closing

Reflection

Families and participants share what they enjoyed, how they felt, and what they would like to experience in future sessions.

Participation

Who Is It For?

Beat & Breeze can be adapted for a wide range of settings and communities. Activities are adjusted based on age, mobility, group size, medical setting, attention level, and available instruments.

Families at Ronald McDonald House Patients and Caregivers Senior Communities Hospital Staff and Care Teams Children and Students Community Organizations Schools Special Education Settings Multigenerational Family Groups
Education

A Session Can Become a Learning Laboratory

Beat & Breeze is also an opportunity for students to study how music affects participation, emotional connection, family interaction, stress, and confidence.

Student leaders may help design surveys, observe participation, collect feedback, revise session plans, create instructional videos, and develop new accessible activities.

In this way, service, education, creativity, and research become part of one experience.
