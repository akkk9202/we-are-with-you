Breeze

Learn through rhythm, breath, movement, and shared music-making.

An in-person teaching program where GYCO students help participants explore music through accessible and interactive activities.

♩
The Program

What Is Beat & Breeze?

Beat & Breeze brings music teaching directly into community spaces.

GYCO students guide participants through rhythm, percussion instruments, breathing, listening, movement, singing, and simple ensemble activities.

Rather than presenting a formal concert, students become teachers and participants become active music-makers.

Every session is designed to be welcoming, flexible, and accessible for people with different levels of musical experience.

The Meaning

The Meaning of the Name

Beat

Rhythm, energy, participation, and the courage to make the first sound. Participants may clap, tap, play percussion instruments, follow patterns, or create their own rhythms.

Breeze

Breathing, movement, calmness, expression, and connection. Participants may practice guided breathing, move with music, sing, listen, or create quiet musical moments together.

Beat and Breeze come together when rhythm gives people energy and breath gives them space to connect.

Curriculum

What We Teach

Rhythm

Students teach participants how to recognize, repeat, and create simple rhythmic patterns.

Percussion

Participants explore tambourines, hand drums, rhythm sticks, triangles, shakers, castanets, glockenspiels, and other accessible instruments.

Breathing

Students guide simple breathing exercises that can be combined with music, movement, or moments of rest.

Listening

Participants learn how to listen for rhythm, melody, musical changes, and other members of the group.

Ensemble Skills

The group learns how to begin together, stop together, follow a leader, and create a shared piece of music.

Creative Expression

Participants may invent rhythms, select instruments, suggest movements, or help lead an activity.

Session Structure

What Happens During a Session?

1

Welcome and Introduction

Students introduce themselves, the instruments, and the purpose of the session. Participants are encouraged to join at their own comfort level.

2

Rhythm Warm-Up

The group begins with clapping, tapping, movement, or simple call-and-response activities.

3

Instrument Exploration

Participants are introduced to different instruments and learn how to hold and play them.

4

Guided Teaching

GYCO students teach a short rhythm, musical pattern, breathing exercise, or song activity.

5

Group Creation

Participants use what they have learned to make music together.

6

Reflection

The group may discuss what they enjoyed, what they learned, and what they would like to try next time.

Participation

Who Can Participate?

Beat & Breeze can be adapted based on age, mobility, group size, energy level, and available space.

Student Growth

Teaching Is Part of Learning

Beat & Breeze also helps GYCO students grow. Students are not only demonstrating what they know — they are learning how to make that knowledge useful to others.

Students learn how to:

  • Explain musical ideas clearly
  • Adapt activities for different participants
  • Lead groups with patience
  • Respond when an activity does not go as planned
  • Listen to and learn from participant feedback
  • Create more accessible teaching methods
