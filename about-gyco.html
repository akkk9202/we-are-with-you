About
GYCO

Young musicians learning how to perform, teach, serve, and lead.

Our History

Our Story

GYCO began in 2023 as a youth chamber orchestra focused on music and community service.

During early performances in senior communities, students saw how familiar songs could awaken memories, encourage conversation, and create moments of joy.

They also discovered that the most meaningful experiences often happened after the music. Students spoke with residents. Community members shared stories. Families joined songs. People responded with memories, gratitude, and encouragement.

Those interactions helped GYCO grow beyond a traditional youth orchestra. Performances became opportunities for connection. Students became teachers. Community visits became learning experiences. Feedback became research. Music projects became educational resources.

The orchestra gradually developed into a student-centered educational community.

Mission

Our Mission

GYCO helps young people develop their abilities through music, teaching, leadership, service, research, and collaboration so they can use what they learn to strengthen their communities.

Vision

Our Vision

We envision a community where students, families, schools, healthcare organizations, senior communities, and local partners learn and grow together through meaningful experiences.

From Our Director

A Note From Our Director

Music education should not end with the ability to perform a piece well.

Students should also learn how to listen to others, communicate with different people, respond to community needs, and use their abilities responsibly.

At GYCO, we hope every rehearsal, performance, teaching experience, and relationship becomes part of a larger education. Our students begin with music, but through music they learn patience, leadership, empathy, creativity, and service.

Our success is not measured only by the quality of a concert. It is also measured by the connections our students build and what they choose to do with what they have learned.

Talent becomes most meaningful when it creates a place for someone else to participate.

Leadership

Leadership Through Responsibility

GYCO students may take meaningful roles based on their interests, abilities, and commitment. Leadership is not simply a title — it is the willingness to prepare, communicate, serve, and follow through.

Planning Performances Leading Rehearsals Teaching Music Mentoring Students Coordinating Volunteers Creating Teaching Videos Designing Websites & Media Collecting Participant Feedback Creating Educational Materials Representing GYCO at Events
How Everything Connects

The Circle of Love

GYCO students learn and prepare.

They share music through performances, teaching videos, and in-person lessons.

Community members participate through singing, learning, requesting songs, or sharing experiences.

Students listen to those responses.

The responses shape new programs and projects.

The work returns to the community.

This ongoing relationship is the Circle of Love.
