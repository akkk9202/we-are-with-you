Our Origin

Music Was Our Beginning

GYCO began with young musicians sharing performances in senior communities.

Through those early experiences, our students discovered that the most meaningful moments often happened beyond the performance itself. They happened when someone recognized a familiar song, shared a memory, joined the music, spoke with a student, or felt seen by another person.

That realization changed the purpose of our work.

Music remained our first tool for connection, but it was no longer only something we performed for others. It became something we could teach, create, experience, and share together.

From that idea, WE ARE WITH YOU began to grow.

Music is not only something we perform. It is something that allows people to meet one another.

Our Philosophy

Connection Begins When Everyone Has a Place to Participate

We believe community outreach should not be one-sided.

Students should not simply arrive, perform, and leave. Patients, families, healthcare professionals, seniors, and community members should also have opportunities to request, learn, sing, play, reflect, and share.

Each program is designed to create a relationship between the person giving and the person receiving.

This relationship forms what we call the Circle of Love.

Our Framework

The Circle of Love

CIRCLE
OF LOVE
Step One

Listen

We begin by learning about the people and communities around us.

↓
Step Two

Share

Students share music, teaching, time, and encouragement.

↓
Step Three

Participate

Patients, families, staff, seniors, and community members are invited to join.

↓
Step Four

Reflect

Participants may share their memories, stories, feedback, and experiences.

↓
Step Five

Continue

Their participation inspires the next performance, lesson, video, or community project.

The Circle of Love does not end when a performance or activity is over. Each experience creates the possibility for another person to learn, participate, share, and feel connected.

Our Approach

No One Is Only an Audience Member

A patient can request the next song.

A family member can sing beside the orchestra.

A healthcare professional can learn a rhythm.

A senior can share the memory connected to a melody.

A student can become a teacher.

A participant can share an experience that shapes the next project.

Everyone enters the Circle of Love with something meaningful to contribute.

Who We Serve

Learning With Our Communities

Our programs are designed to grow through genuine relationships. We do not enter a community believing that we already have every answer. We begin by listening.

  • Patients and caregivers
  • Families staying near hospitals
  • Healthcare professionals and hospital staff
  • Senior living communities
  • Students and educators
  • Faith and community organizations
  • Volunteers and partner organizations
  • Musicians of different ages and experience levels
