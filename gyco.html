Where Our Journey Began

A student-centered musical community where young people learn, perform, lead, serve, and create together.

♬
Our Purpose

More Than an Orchestra

GYCO brings students together through music, but performance is only the beginning of the experience.

Students rehearse and perform while also learning how to organize projects, mentor peers, communicate with community partners, conduct research, create educational materials, and serve people from different generations and backgrounds.

GYCO provides the first shared space where students discover that their talents can become tools for connection and community impact.

The Bigger Picture

A Musician Can Become More Than a Performer

Through GYCO, students may discover interests in healthcare, education, research, technology, communication, nonprofit leadership, publishing, or community development.

We do not expect every student to pursue music professionally.

We hope every student learns how to use their abilities with discipline, empathy, creativity, and purpose.

Our History

Our Story

GYCO began in 2023 as a youth chamber orchestra focused on performance and community service.

During early visits to senior communities, students saw that familiar music could awaken memories, encourage conversation, and create joy. They also learned that the most meaningful moments often happened after the final note, when students and community members spoke, listened, and connected.

Those experiences expanded GYCO's understanding of music education. Rehearsals became places for collaboration. Performances became opportunities for service. Community visits became opportunities for research and reflection. Students became project leaders, mentors, educators, and creators.

What began as a youth orchestra gradually became a student-centered educational community.

Mission

Our Mission

From Our Director

A Note From Our Director

Music education should not end with the ability to perform a piece well.

Students should also learn how to listen to others, work with different people, respond to community needs, and use their abilities with responsibility.

At GYCO, we hope every rehearsal, performance, project, and relationship becomes part of a larger education. Our students begin with music, but through music they learn leadership, patience, empathy, creativity, and service.

Our greatest success is not measured only by the quality of a concert. It is measured by what students choose to do with what they have learned.

The purpose of talent is not only personal achievement. It is the ability to create opportunities for others.

Leadership

Student Leadership in Action

Students are encouraged to take meaningful roles based on their strengths and interests. Leadership is not treated as a title — it is demonstrated through responsibility, consistency, communication, and service.

Planning Performances Leading Rehearsals Teaching Younger Students Coordinating Volunteers Managing Media & Communications Creating Research Tools Designing Websites Developing Community Projects Representing GYCO in Partner Meetings
Our Community

Our Community

GYCO's work has included relationships with healthcare organizations, family support organizations, senior communities, schools, faith communities, educators, researchers, volunteers, and global educational partners.

We value partnerships that are built through trust, listening, and shared purpose.

Our goal is not to enter a community with a finished answer. Our goal is to learn alongside the community and create something meaningful together.
