Greater Youth Collaborative Opus

GYCO

Music is where we begin. Connection is how we grow.

A student-centered orchestra and educational community where young musicians learn, perform, teach, lead, serve, and create together.

♬
Our Purpose

More Than an Orchestra

GYCO brings students together through music. Students develop musical skills through rehearsals, coaching, collaboration, and performance. However, their growth is not limited to the concert stage.

They also learn how to teach, lead groups, communicate with community partners, design projects, conduct research, create educational resources, and respond to real community needs.

GYCO is where students first discover that their talents can become tools for connection.

Student Experience

What Students Experience

Learn

Develop musicianship through practice, rehearsals, coaching, listening, and collaboration.

Perform

Share music through concerts, community events, senior outreach, and special projects.

Teach

Use musical knowledge to create in-person lessons and teaching videos.

Lead

Plan projects, mentor students, communicate with partners, and take responsibility.

Serve

Build genuine relationships with patients, families, seniors, staff, and community members.

Reflect

Study experiences, collect feedback, and improve future programs.

Create

Develop new music activities, videos, resources, research, and community projects.

The Connection

GYCO and WE ARE WITH YOU

WE ARE WITH YOU grew from the experiences of GYCO students. As students performed in community spaces, they began to ask new questions.

How could participants become more involved?

How could students teach instead of only perform?

How could music reach people who could not attend in person?

How could a requested song become a shared experience?

Those questions led to Voices of Love, Beat & Breeze, Taps of Love, Winds of Love, and the Circle of Love.

Rehearsals, performances, student leadership, and WE ARE WITH YOU activities.

Our Goal

Begin With Music.
Grow Through Service.

We do not expect every GYCO student to become a professional musician. We hope every student learns how to use their abilities with discipline, empathy, creativity, and purpose.
