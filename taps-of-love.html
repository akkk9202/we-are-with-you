of Love

Music learning, shared one video at a time.

Short teaching videos created by GYCO students to help people learn rhythm, instruments, breathing, singing, and music activities wherever they are.

▶
The Program

What Is Taps of Love?

Not everyone can attend an in-person music session.

Some participants may be at home, receiving treatment, staying in a hospital, living far away, or unable to join a scheduled activity.

Taps of Love allows GYCO students to send music teaching directly to them.

Through short and accessible videos, students demonstrate rhythms, instruments, breathing exercises, sing-along activities, and beginner music lessons.

Participants can watch, pause, repeat, and practice at their own pace.

Why It Matters

Learning Should Be Able to Travel

A teaching video can reach someone even when students cannot be there in person. The video is not intended to replace a teacher — it is intended to make the first step easier.

It can provide:

  • A simple activity during a long hospital stay
  • A music lesson for a family at home
  • A rhythm exercise for a classroom
  • A breathing activity for a quiet moment
  • An instrument introduction for a beginner
  • A sing-along experience
  • A connection between students and distant communities
What We Create

Video Categories

Rhythm Taps

Short lessons involving clapping, tapping, counting, and repeating patterns.

Instrument Taps

Introductions to percussion instruments and other orchestra instruments.

Breath Taps

Simple breathing and movement activities connected to music.

Sing-Along Taps

Songs with lyrics, visual cues, or easy participation instructions.

Family Taps

Activities that children, parents, siblings, and caregivers can complete together.

Community Taps

Videos designed for classrooms, senior communities, healthcare staff, or group activities.

Video Structure

What Each Video Includes

1

A Friendly Welcome

The student introduces themselves and explains what the viewer will learn.

2

One Clear Skill

Each video focuses on one simple concept or activity.

3

A Demonstration

The student slowly demonstrates the activity.

4

Time to Practice

The viewer is invited to repeat the rhythm, movement, note, or song.

5

A Small Challenge

The viewer may be encouraged to try the activity faster, slower, louder, softer, or with another person.

6

A Closing Invitation

The student invites the viewer to continue practicing or share their experience.

Our Standard

Accessible Learning

Taps of Love videos are designed to make beginning feel possible. Viewers should never feel that they have failed if they cannot complete an activity immediately.

Each video should be:

  • Short and focused on one skill at a time
  • Clear and beginner-friendly
  • Encouraging rather than evaluative
  • Easy to pause, repeat, and practice
  • Visually understandable
  • Available with captions when possible
  • Appropriate for different ages and settings
