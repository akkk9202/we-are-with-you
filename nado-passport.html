NADO begins with a personal decision:

"I want to learn too."

"I can participate too."

"I can teach someone too."

"I can help another person begin too."

NADO is not limited to students who are already advanced musicians. It is an invitation for anyone to become a beginner, document their growth, share what they know, and pass opportunity forward.

The Program

What Is NADO Passport?

NADO Passport is a one-year learning journey where participants explore new instruments or skills, learn from peer mentors, teach their own strengths to others, record their progress, and complete the journey by helping someone else begin.

The project grew from Beat & Breeze and its belief that everyone can participate in music.

NADO Passport turns that belief into a repeatable educational system. It is not a one-time challenge. It is a structure that can continue through future participants, families, schools, healthcare communities, and global partners.

The Journey

The Core Journey

Learn

Become a beginner again. Explore a new instrument, skill, culture, or method of learning.

Teach

Share something you already understand. Every participant is both a learner and a mentor.

Document

Record progress through videos, reflections, lessons, photographs, and passport stamps.

Pass It Forward

Help another person begin through mentoring, educational content, instrument access, or donation.

The final stamp is not a reward for yourself. It represents someone else's new beginning.

How It Works

The Passport System

Step 1

Watch and Learn

Participants use short, accessible instructional videos.

Step 2

Practice and Reflect

Participants document challenges, discoveries, and progress.

Step 3

Collect Stamps

Each stamp represents participation, courage, growth, teaching, or service.

Step 4

Create Learning Content

Participants help improve the materials for the next learner.

Step 5

Pass It Forward

The journey ends by opening an opportunity for someone else.

The Mission

An Instrument Is More Than a Gift

The Pass It Forward system connects learning with responsibility.

At the end of a NADO journey, participants may help provide an instrument, learning resource, mentorship opportunity, or educational connection to another learner.

The purpose is not simply to donate an object. The purpose is to provide:

  • → A reason to begin
  • → Someone willing to teach
  • → Accessible learning content
  • → A way to document growth
  • → A future opportunity to give back
◈
Accessibility

Video Learning System

NADO Passport content is designed for people who may not have access to private lessons, large music programs, or advanced equipment. Each video lesson should be:

Short

Focused on one skill at a time, easy to pause and return to.

Clear

Beginner-friendly and visually understandable without prior knowledge.

Accessible

Available with subtitles, usable at home or in a community setting.

Expandable

Participants contribute their own lessons, translations, and reflections.

The Philosophy

We are not sending performances. We are sending the courage to begin.

NADO Passport celebrates the humility of becoming a beginner. It teaches that expertise does not separate people — it creates a responsibility to help others learn.

Every participant teaches what they know and learns what they do not know yet.

The final stamp is not for yourself. It is for someone else to begin.
